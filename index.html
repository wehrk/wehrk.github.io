But why not
one turn more?

A seashell's spiral has no natural stopping point. Its form is one structure repeated past any need for it, past the point where the repetition itself becomes the only content.

Explored live, across a spatial field, how long a thing can remain what it is before it becomes something else, or nothing at all.

Surprise

A rupture in a stable profile.

The moment an interpretation stops holding, and another has to begin.

What startles must come from outside the self.

Charm

Many possibilities held at once, before one resolves.

Unity and plurality, perceptible at the same time.

A delay in resolution and surplus, revealed through suspension.

Repetition

Difference within sameness.

The same returns, but the moment has changed.

An open spiral, each turn driven by what the last left behind.
